Accesibility

The main problem that accessibility faces nowadays is the lack of knowledge of the current policies in society. The first step to solve this problem is to be aware that this problem exists!

The objective of physical accessibility is to achieve equal access to every space independently of our physical characteristics or capabilities. How is the experience for a kid in a school if his environment does not even allow him to enter the institution without any assistance because of physical barriers? What if the ilumination of a classroom doesn't facilitate the reading of what is being taught?

Knowing the actual policies and -over all things- recognising the problems in our surroundings, society can builde accessible spaces for everyone

Arbility

Arbility is a simulation tool that, through Augmented Reality, allows the evaluation of physical accessibility.

Arbility is centered in the educational environment, allowing the creation of teacher and student profiles. The teachers are able to create routes defining potential accessibility obstacles for their students to evaluate. Appart from generating awareness of the existing problems and policies in the younger generations, the schools will be able to detect faults in their accessibility plans through an automatically generated report.

But arbility is also meant to be used by the rest of the users through a guest profile where any person will have access to all evaluation tools aswell as the augmented reality simulator.

Arbility is intended to bring augmented reality and accessibility toghether to create a deep societal awareness of accessibility issues in everyday spaces.

Doors

A door is accessible if:
• Its width is greater than 80 centimeters.
• Its height is greater than 200 centimeters.
• The height of its opening mechanism is between 80 centimeters and 120 centimeters.

To perform these measurements, Arbility uses its augmented reality measurement tools.

Devices compatible with ARCore

Ilumination

A space is correctly iluminated if:
• While in the exterior the luxometer registers more than 100 lux.
• While in an interior space with ramps or stairs the luxometer registers a value between 150 and 200 lux.
• While in an habitable interior the luxometer registers more than 300 lux.

To perform these measurements, Arbility uses the phone's luminescence sensor.

Lifts

A lift is accessible if:
• Its width is greater than 110 centimeters.
• The depth of the lift car is greater than 140 centimeters.
• The height of the curb between the floor and the lift car is less than 1 centimeter.
• The distance between the floor and the lift car is less than 2 centimeters.
• It has an automatic opening and closing mechanism.
• It has an audible opening and closing signal.
• It has a braille button pad.

To perform these measurements, Arbility uses its augmented reality measurement tools and simple questions to the user for observable characteristics

Devices compatible with ARCore

Counters

A counter is accessible if:
• The width of the working space is greater than 80 centimeters.
• The height of the working space is less than 85 centimeters.
• The height of the interior free space is greater than 70 centimeters.
• The depth of the interior free space is greater than 50 centimeters.

To perform these measurements, Arbility uses its augmented reality measurement tools.

Devices compatible with ARCore

Ramps

A ramp is accessible if:
• Its width is greater than 120 centimeters.
• While being less than 3 meters long the slope is less or equal than 10.
• While being between 3 and 6 meters long the slope is less or equal than 8.
• While being between 6 and 9 meters long the slope is less or equal than 6.
• The hight of the superior handrail is between 85 and 100 centimeters.
• The hight of the inferior handrail is between 60 and 75 centimeters.
• The maneuvering diameter is greater than 150 centimeters.

To perform these measurements, Arbility uses its augmented reality measurement tools aswell as the information obtained by the device's inclinometer sensor.

Devices compatible with ARCore

Stairlifts

A stairlift is accessible if:
• The width of the platform is greater than 70 centimeters.
• The depth of the platform is greater than 100 centimeters.
• There are controls in the platform while boarding and unboarding.
• The allowed weight is greater than 250kg.
• The speed is greater than 0,15 m/s.

To perform these measurements, Arbility uses its augmented reality measurement tools and simple questions to the user for observable characteristics

Devices compatible with ARCore

Emergencies

The evacuation parameters are met if:
• There are emergency drills.
• There is emergency lighting.

Simulation

The simulator uses augmented reality to project a 3D model of a wheelchair with customizable dimensions.

Thanks to this projection and the ability to move it freely, allowing turns and acceleration, the simulator serves its purpose as a tool to visualize in a quick way a space's accessibility for wheelchair users in an intuitive and natural way.

Devices compatible with ARCore

Accessible design

The high contrast of the interface and the meticulous user experience design make Arbility an application focused in accessibility in all fields.

Free access

With the "guest" profile, any user can evaluate the accessibility of obstacles without the need to register in Arbility, allowing a completely free and anonymous access.

Simulation with Augmented Reality

Besides from the measurements made with augmented reality, Arbility includes a simulator to project over any space a 3D model of a wheelchair with custom dimensions and totally controllable in all directions.

Focus on education

Arbility is focused on serving as a tool for schools where teachers will be able to draft routes with potential accessibility obstacles which their students will be able to measure, generating thus accessibility information for the school and a strong sense of awareness in the students from a young age.
